Learning Patience Takes Time

Being a parent is most definitely one of the most important patience tests. They say patience is a virtue... which is true... but this comment makes you think patience is a quality that only a few chosen ones have. In fact, patience is learned over time even if some parents don’t do well on this chapter at first. But, with a little effort, any parent can learn to be patient and to amuse themselves over some situations that not so long ago annoyed them.

First you have to learn to have patience with yourself. Don't blame yourself for everything and show tolerance. Happily for children, adults are mature persons and know, most of the times, to keep their nerves under control. Don’t raise your voice and impose your wills with a calm but firm tone, no matter the problem.

Children sometimes seem to try on purpose to test the limits of their parents’ patience. If you feel like this is what your little one does, ask yourself "what might push them to this?" Maybe they're trying to cover some frustration, or want revenge for a moment when you were unfair. Or maybe your child just feels neglected and is only trying to get your attention.

To make your child become a patient person you can:

* Play with them games that require taking turns - this will teach them to wait until his turn comes.

* Read to him thick books and long stories, leaving some to follow-up for the next evening. Do not give in to pleads to find out the ending in the same evening.

* Teach them to plant a tree or to take care of a flower - they will learn to be patient until they see the results of their work.

* If a child wants a bike, give money a little at a time, and tell them to save up for that bike. Of course you will be the one that will give them the present, but your child will learn to have patience and, at the same time, the value of money.
