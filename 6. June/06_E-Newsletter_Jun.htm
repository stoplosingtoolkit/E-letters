*** A Minnesota Story ***

All his life Ole had heard about an amazing family tradition. His father, grandfather and great-grandfather had all been able to walk on water on their 21st birthdays. They walked across the lake on their way to the boat club for their first legal drink.

When his 21st birthday came around, Ole and his pal took a boat to the middle of the lake. Ole stepped out of the boat and nearly drowned!

Confused, he went to see his grandmother. He asked, "Grandma, it’s my 21st birthday, why can’t I walk across the lake like my father, his father, and his father too?"

Granny looked into Ole's eyes and said, "But their birthdays were in January.
Yours is in June!"

How to Attract Birds to Your Garden

First off … birds will be attracted by the geographic location of your garden. To find out which birds frequent your area, consult the specialty magazines and the internet. Libraries and book stores have books based on migration and on the routes that some bird species take to get to their summer or winter residence.

Birds need water. This is highly important. A bath or a pool, no matter the size or the material, would instantly attract many bird families, even wild ducks and geese.

If you could also arrange a place where they could find some food, bird seeds, bread or anything else they might like, that would increase the chances they made an appearance in your garden. (Almost all birds prefer sunflower seeds, which are extremely easy to find and inexpensive.) Before buying the seeds, you should make a list with all the birds that are in the area and with every bird’s food preferences to ensure you can provide food for many types of birds present.

Another thing to consider is to keep away from the garden all the things that might scare or disturb birds.

Animals like the neighbor’s dogs can scare the birds. Try and ask the neighbors to keep the dogs chained or in the house, because many birds fear dogs.

Also, you must know that some birds do not agree with each other, so that a certain species might avoid your garden because there is another species in it.

Some can also stay away because of the fact you have children and the noise is too loud, and others can stay away because the place doesn’t look natural enough.

Bottom line with all this is to do a bit of study to know what birds like and don’t like … their nature and habits… it all works together to bring our feathered friends to the backyard.
