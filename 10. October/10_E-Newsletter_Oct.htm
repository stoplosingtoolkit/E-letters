The Lighter Side...


Great truths little children have learned

1) No matter how hard you try, you can't baptize cats.

2. If your sister hits you, don't hit her back. They always catch the second person.

3. You can't trust dogs to watch your food.

4. Don't sneeze when someone is cutting your hair.

5. You can't hide a piece of broccoli in a glass of milk.

6. The best place to be when you're sad is Grandpa's lap.

1. Raising teenagers is like nailing Jell-O to a tree.

2. Wrinkles don't hurt.

4. Today's mighty oak is just yesterday's nut that held its ground.

Planning Your Child’s Study Area

Once your child starts school, there are several required changes in their room’s layout. Now, a study place is a must, a place where the little scholar can prepare for school, learn and do his or her homework.

The study place has to be regularly near the window so that his arm will not place a shadow on the books in front of him. According to this rule, if we are talking about a right-handed child, the light must be on the left, and if talking about a left-handed child, the light must be on the right.

The furniture must include a desk and some small cabinets or a small bookcase.

The study table or desk must have at least 24" X 35" and its height and inclination has to be adjustable. There must be some kind of drawers next to it or in it to deposit books, papers, pens and other school stuff.

For this same purpose, the parents must consider buying a bookcase or a cabinet for books, which fits the room and the height of your child. For an optimal use of space, a desk with a study table and some shelves can be chosen.

Natural lighting has to be supplemented or maintained by a local light. This is why the desk must have on it a lamp with an adjustable arm, placed in such a way that it can focalize the light precisely to the point it is needed. The chair must have an adjustable height, fitting the height of the young scholar.

Of course a computer is a necessary object in the child’s room, even in the early student’s room. Used either for fun or study, it has to become a familiar instrument for the little one.

An "L" shaped desk allows a good position for the computer, with the monitor on one side or in the corner. Be careful because the distance from the monitor to the eyes of the person using the computer has to be of 20" - 27". To better respect this distance, some desks are equipped with a special space for the monitor, positioned a little higher than the actual desk.
